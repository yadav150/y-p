About Me

I am Yadav Subba — a son, a brother, a developer, a mathematician, a teacher, and a student of language. I belong to Dikhlem Nepali Subba Gaon, a small village in Hawaipur, West Karbi Anglong, Assam. It is from this humble ground that I have built my dreams — one line of code, one equation, one word at a time.

In the summer of 2020, I lost my father, the sole pillar of my family. I was seventeen and a half, standing at the threshold of adulthood, when grief came not as a visitor but as a permanent resident. Yet from that silence, I forged a new language — the language of code, of logic, of creation. I taught myself to build not just websites, but a future — line by line, lesson by lesson, tear by tear.

Today, I am a self-taught frontend developer with over two years of experience managing a job update portal. I hold a Bachelor of Arts in English (Honours) — my results await, but my skills are already proven. I write with precision, I teach with patience, and I build with purpose.

My hearing impairment has shaped the way I communicate — not as a limitation, but as a lens. My words are my voice, and they speak with clarity, depth, and literary grace. I believe that communication is not just about speaking; it is about being understood.
