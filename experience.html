Professional Experience

Job Update Portal

Manager & Lead Developer

2022 – 2024 • Two Years and More

For over two years, I managed, maintained, and developed a comprehensive job update portal — a platform designed to connect job seekers with opportunities across various sectors. I was entrusted with the full responsibility of the website: from frontend design to backend logic, from content curation to user experience optimisation. I debugged with patience, I fixed with precision, and I improved with purpose.

The portal served as a lifeline for many — a place where hope met opportunity. I did not merely maintain a website; I nurtured a community. Every update I published, every feature I refined, was an act of service. I knew that somewhere, someone was using my work to change their own life — and that knowledge gave me purpose.

Current Status: The website's domain has expired due to financial constraints. For now, it rests in silence. But I have not abandoned it. When I am able, I will renew it — because the work is not finished, and the need remains. The portal is not dead; it is waiting.

Yadav Web Tools

Founder & Lead Developer

2024 – Present

I am the creator and sole developer of Yadav Web Tools — a collection of twelve free, client-side utility tools designed to simplify everyday digital tasks. From age calculation to QR code generation, from GST calculation to password generation, each tool is built with a singular philosophy: privacy-first, client-side, and free.

Every line of code in this project is written with intention. There are no frameworks, no external dependencies, no unnecessary bloat. Just vanilla HTML, CSS, and JavaScript — clean, fast, and reliable. The tools run entirely in the user's browser. No data is transmitted, no privacy is compromised, and no trust is broken.

This project is not just a technical achievement — it is a gift. A gift from someone who knows what it means to struggle, and who believes that technology should serve people, not confuse them. It is my small but honest contribution to the world.

"I have managed, maintained, built, and deployed. I have debugged, fixed, and improved. I have worked not for recognition, but for impact. And I will continue to do so — because the work is never truly finished."
